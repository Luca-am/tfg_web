Abstract i paraules clau

  • AbstractWithin neoliberal governance contexts in contemporary Italy, branding operates as a socio-cultural interface that extracts social and symbolic capital from relational labour in media circulation and consumption. The article analyzes how members of the upper classes, including actors linked to organized crime and institutional politics, use media self-branding to consolidate connective leadership and reproduce class privilege.
